12 Best Places To Visit During Summer In Canada


If you wish to spend a fun packed summer in the soothing land of Canada, here’s a list of places you ought to visit for that added thrill to your vacay!

    1. Georgian Bay



Having the Bruce Trail on board, which is known to be Canada’s longest footpath and also amongst the oldest ones too, the Georgian bay has multiple activities for every kind of traveler. Whether you wish to go for a soothing canoeing in the waters or hike down the trails here, there’s always room for more fun in this place. Visitors prefer hiring a glass bottom boat to venture into the wilds of Flowerpot Island and are left with awe-inspiring views amid the mysterious Collingwood caves. You wouldn’t be surprised if a summer in Canada feels well spent in this paradise!


    2. Grand Bend



A holiday filled with calming warm waters and serene sandy beaches is what you’ll find in Grand Bend in Ontario. Whether it’s a visit to the farmer’s market here or the Lambton Heritage Museum, this place is lined up with exciting things to do on your summer holiday in Canada. Try a banana boat ride or parasailing on the beach to witness the splendid views of this place from up above. Staying in some of the resorts here is surely a good idea but if you wish to be a little more adventurous, try pitching a tent at one of the campsites or rent a cottage for those cozy vibes.


    3. Eastern Townships Of Quebec



Also known as the cottage country of Montreal, Eastern townships of Quebec comprise of a series of picturesque villages and towns where you get to pamper the foodie in your in all the best ways possible. While there are a number of activities to try here, going to the Ski Bromont water park can lead to an amazing experience of a lifetime. You can buy a few homemade chocolates around the region to go for and get going on a serene expedition towards Mont-Orford National Park here. Even kids can enjoy a good time in the Zoo de Granby of this area.


    4. Victoria



A perfect reviving spot, away from the city life is Victoria in British Columbia. If you’re on a family vacation, be sure to check out the butterfly gardens where kids can enjoy the company of some beautiful flamingos, other birds and butterflies. As there’s no shortage of grand castles and ancient churches in the region, try visiting a few to witness the remarkable work of fine Victorian architecture. A long flight of stairs in Craigdarroch Castle Tower will guide you to a panoramic view of this city. This place will surely offer you some unique experiences for spending you summer holidays in Canada in 2022.


    5. Prince Edward Island



If you haven’t been to a place which feels like a dreamy paradise, well, now is the perfect time to head to Prince Edward Island in Canada for that perfect feel. The beautiful old lighthouses are dotted along the island along with red sands on the beaches that welcomes your feet with a pleasant weather up above. Try clam digging here in those zippy wetsuits or simply go for a lobster tour boat to find out how you can fish a lobster in the vast region. There are plenty of activities for kids too, including the ones in Shining Waters Family Fun Park where they can go for water slides, meet mini horses and alpacas and sail a small pirate boat of their own.


    6. Halifax



Be it a soothing walk on the boardwalk or a tour down the Halifax Public Gardens for a fun picnic by the flowers, Halifax has so much in store for every kind of traveler. Even the kids can enjoy the ship-themed playground here or visit the Discovery centre that offers a great learning experience. It is said that visiting Peggy’s Cove is mandatory while you’re headed to Halifax for exploring the famous stops in this region. The spellbinding shores of this village are surely a must visit.


    7. Ottawa



Boasting of multiple museums and galleries, this famous hub of history and culture offers some unique experiences like chilling with some dinosaurs in Canadian Museum of Nature and get ready to pamper the water baby in you at Calypso Park. Don’t forget to make a mandatory stop at Valleyview Little Animal Farm and meet the baby goats, ducklings and pet rabbits if you’re fond of animals. Try the various cuisines at the ByWard Market in Ottawa for digging in the delicacies that this place is famous for.


    8. Niagara Falls




Any trip to Canada is undoubtedly incomplete without a visit to the renowned Niagara falls. Booking a boat trip to these majestic falls including Horseshoe Falls and American Falls. Head straight to The Flying Saucer Restaurant for an amazing diner experience. If you’re a nature lover, the Butterfly Conservatory will be just the kind of place you’d want to visit for a blissful experience. While beating the heat in summer is the usual thing you would want to do, the Great Wolf Lodge will be perfect for all the activities you’re looking forward to.


    9. Mont Tremblant




Yet another spot where you can go try various excursions and activities to keep up with the thrillist in you. Go for a hiking tour amid the Laurentian Mountains or try cycling around. If you simply wish to soak in more bliss and witness some splendid views of the place, get a gondola ride. Families can enjoy some bungee-jumping together at Eurobungy or visit Akropark, which is prefered by kids. An ice-cream treat at the end of the day is a must with all the fun activities in the region, so make sure you try the one with maple butter at Sugar Shack.


    10. Corner Brook



One of the perks of spending your summer holidays in Canada in 2022 is that you can explore as many places as you wish to, all fitting into one perfect destination. Corner Brook is known to be amongst the topmost historical places in the entire Bay of Islands. While you’re in this region of Newfoundland, check out the Newfoundland Hockey Hall of Fame and get a lesson on some of the greatest players of eastern Canada. You can also walk along Corner Brook Stream Trail, guiding you to the Glynmill Inn Pond to spot some beautiful swans.


    11. Montreal



With almost all the streets made up of cobblestone, you’ll find yourself falling in love with the European essence of this place in Canada. A visit to the Jean-Doré Beach can lead to a fun kayaking experience whether you’re traveling solo or with your family. If this isn’t what you had in mind, go for a more adventurous rollercoaster ride at Six Flags’ La Ronde. Head to the Lachine Canal, for a serene bike ride and watch as you slowly pedal along the way, enjoying the Canadian air all the way. Lined with many bakeries and restaurants, don’t forget to stop by at Atwater Market for a quick taste of gelato!


    12. Alta Range


If you’re planning to visit Canada in summer, Alta range will surely make your experience worthwhile. With the true historic cowboy vibes at the Cowboy Trail, you can go rafting, fishing, and horse-riding. Staying won’t be a hassle here as the Red Deer River Ranch offers a comfortable stay to all the travelers. Activities like biking and hiking will turn out to be more fun with the mesmerizing views leading towards the south region. For an entirely unique experience amid this scenic range, book a guided tour for learning more about the life of a coal miner, grab your mining gear and head to the Bellevue Underground Mine.
